Hane Board: Puzzle Training System

Completed 2026 C++ Platform IO Mechanical Electrical 3D Printing

A tactile puzzle training system designed to prepare neurodivergent workers for the fast-paced Food & Beverage industry by breaking down complex workflows.

Specifications

Microcontroller

ESP32-S3

I/O Expansion

PCF8574 (I2C Protocol)

Actuation

Solenoids driven by MOSFETs

Sensing

LDR (Light Dependent Resistor) Matrix

Bridging the Gap in Training

Flour Power SG is an inclusive F&B organisation that trains and employs persons with disabilities. Through this collaboration, we found that neurodivergent workers can find traditional training methods overwhelming. Hané Board turns F&B workflows into physical puzzle-based tasks, creating an engaging way for users to build muscle memory, stay motivated, work independently, and reduce supervisor workload.

System testing with target users.

The Concept: Workflow

01

Receive Order

The cashier inputs an order, and Hané Board releases the matching puzzle piece for the task into the tray.

02

Guided Assembly

The worker collects the piece and views a clear, step-by-step visual breakdown on the digital UI to complete the physical task.

03

Satisfying Snap

After completing the task, the worker snaps the piece back into the board. The system records the completion and updates their colour mastery level, helping them track progress and stay motivated.

Electrical Architecture

  • ESP32-S3 Main Controller: Controls the full system, including puzzle detection, solenoid dispensing, and communication with the web-based UI.
  • PCF8574 I2C GPIO Expander: Expands the ESP32-S3’s available pins to connect multiple LDR sensors.
  • LDR Detection Matrix: Each puzzle slot uses an LDR to sense light changes. When a piece blocks the light, the system detects it as placed; when light reaches the sensor, it detects that the piece is removed.
  • Solenoids (6V, 2A): Solenoids are used to physically release the puzzle pieces into the tray when a task is assigned.
  • FQP30N06L N-channel MOSFET: Acts as a high-current switch, allowing the ESP32-S3 to safely control the 6V 2A solenoids without powering them directly.
  • 1N4007 flyback diode: Solenoids are inductive loads that create a dangerous voltage spike when switched off. A flyback diode is placed across each solenoid to absorb this kickback and protect the ESP32-S3 from electrical damage.
  • 100µF decoupling capacitor: Decoupling capacitors are added near the solenoid power circuits to smooth out sudden current drops when the solenoids activate, keeping the logic-level electronics stable.

Mechanical Integration

Version 1.0

  • Frame Connection Tabs: Small rectangular tabs were added to connect the central mechanism securely to the outer frame.
  • Print-Sized Middle Plate:The central plate was made smaller so it could fit within the Bambu 3D printer’s build volume, making the part easier to fabricate.
  • Reinforcement Ribs: Structural ribs were added across the frame to strengthen the connections and reduce flexing during use.

Version 2.0

  • Diagonal Side-Constrained Connectors: The small rectangular connection tabs were moved to the sides to better constrain both vertical and horizontal movement.
  • Stronger Central Mounting Area: The middle frame was designed as a more solid structure to strengthen the mounting points for the LDR sensors and solenoids.
  • Optimized Light Isolation Wells: Empty spaces were kept around the sides of the central frame to allow easier wiring and electronics connection.

Engineering Challenges

Weak Puzzle Dispensing Force

The Problem

During prototyping, we tested electromagnets to release the components, but the repelling force generated was far too weak to push out the magnetic puzzle pieces from their holding tracks.

The Solution

We pivoted to solenoids. The physical plunger action provided a much stronger, positive mechanical push that cleared the track smoothly for piece dispensing.

Strong Magnetic Resistance

The Problem

The magnets embedded inside the physical puzzle pieces were too strong when resting directly against the metal dispensing core, making it difficult for the solenoid plunger to push out the piece.

The Solution

We 3D-printed a custom internal spacer to create a distance between the solenoid face and the puzzle piece magnet, reducing the magnet strength to allow puzzle piece dispensing.

Solenoid Power Constraints

The Problem

We chose 6V solenoids to keep the system compatible with common power-bank options, but we overlooked the increase in current. Our power bank could not supply 2A directly.

The Solution

We added FQP30N06L MOSFET drivers to act as high-current switches, allowing the microcontroller to safely turn on the solenoids using a separate power path.

Ambient Light Interference

The Problem

The LDR sensor matrix was highly vulnerable to shifting room lighting environments, causing inconsistent puzzle placement detection.

The Solution

We tuned the LDR module’s sensitivity using its built-in variable resistor and added a 2-second confirmation delay in code before registering a puzzle piece as fully returned.
